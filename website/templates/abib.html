Annotated Bibliography

  1. Adler, P. (2009). Topographical Mapping. In Mapping New York (pp. 142-146). London: Black Dog Pub.

    Features different types of mapping used by New York city dating all the way back to the mid 1600’s. Focuses on specific realms of mapping including crime, poverty, topography, data, transport, and commerce. Included many images and representations of what they maps looked like and highlighted their most influential aspects.

  2. Briffault, Richard. "Voting Rights, Home Rule, and Metropolitan Governance: The Secession of Staten Island as a Case Study in the Dilemmas of Local Self-Determination." Columbia Law Review (n.d.): n. pag.

    This journal article details an attempt by the borough of Staten Island to secede from New York City in the 1990s. The journal discusses the neglect felt by the borough from the New York City government, as well as social and demographic differences with the rest of the city. Although the article is more concerned with the overall issue of home rule, it spotlights an area of New York City that differs from the city’s character and is often neglected in discussions about New York.

  3. Chronopoulos, T. (2013). Robert Moses and the Visual Dimension of Physical Disorder: Efforts to Demonstrate Urban Blight in the Age of Slum Clearance. Journal of Planning History, Vol. 13(3) 207-233. Retrieved February 10, 2015, from http://jph.sagepub.com/content/13/3/207.full.pdf

    This academic article focuses on efforts of Robert Moses, one of the most influential planners in New York history, to remove urban blight in New York City in the 1950s. The article discusses his visions for removal of blight, as well as its documentation. This source is useful for understanding a more detailed piece of a New York City urban planning issue that was handled by a powerful figure in the city’s history.

  4. DCP History Project - New York City Department of City Planning. (n.d.). Retrieved February12,2015, from http://www.nyc.gov/html/dcp/html/history_project/history_project3.shtml

    This article talks about the history of City Planning Commission as well as a concise overview ofdevelopment of New York City. In the meanwhile, the article illustrates the decision that the City Planning Commission had made during the past few years such as Zoning Ordinance, etc.

  5. Doig, J. (2001). Empire on the Hudson: Entrepreneurial vision and political power at the Port of New York Authority (pp. 20-80). New York: Columbia University Press.

    Discussed the history of the ferry system within New York City starting with the informal use of rowboats and horse-powered boats. Went on to provide detail about the operating systems, routes, and overall usage of such ferry systems across the Hudson. Looked especially at how political leaders and players in the planning process manipulated such systems to garner more economic power and commerce expansion.

  6. Downs, A. (1981). Neighborhoods and urban development. Washington, DC: The Brookings Institution.

    A deep study in the socio-economic influences of urban development on the welfare of the community. This source is useful as it provides an objective point of view on the reasons behind neighborhoods experiencing blight.

  7. Fischler, R. (2007). The Metropolitan Dimension of Early Zoning Revisiting the 1916 New York City Ordinance. Journal of the American Planning Association, 64(2). Retrieved February 11, 2015, from http://www.tandfonline.com/doi/pdf/10.1080/01944369808975974

    This article discusses the New York City Ordinance of 1916, the first comprehensive zoning ordinance in a major American city. The article discusses the years and events leading up to its implementation, notably uncontrolled building practices and congestion. This article details with a major event in New York City and American planning history, and its mentioning is important in any discussion of New York’s history.

  8. Garner, D. (2009, August 4). When David Fought Goliath in Washington Square Park. The New York Times. Retrieved March 7, 2015.

    This New York Times article discusses Jane Jacobs’s fierce opposition to some of Robert Moses’s proposed projects. This source was primarily used to discuss her success as an advocate for the preservation of urban neighborhoods and historic districts. This source most notably includes her stopping of three megaprojects that would have changed the landscape of some areas of New York City.

  9. Gentrification. (n.d.). In Merriam-Webster. Retrieved from http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/gentrification

    Definition of gentrification. This source is useful in explaining the negative effects of redevelopment and reinvestment.

  10. Hall, P. (1988). Cities of tomorrow: An intellectual history of urban planning and design in the twentieth century. Oxford, UK: Blackwell.

    This book Cities of Tomorrow includes the history of planning in the twentieth century and also the problems happening in the society. It gives an overview of the evolution of the planning movement in the twentieth century.

  11. History of Brooklyn - From Village to City. (n.d.). Retrieved March 3, 2015, from http://www.thirteen.org/brooklyn/history/history3.html

    This narrative came from the website Thirteen WNET New York, a local network known colloquially as “Channel Thirteen.” This work gives a general overview of the history of Brooklyn before its consolidation with the City of New York, from the founding of Brooklyn’s towns to its rise as a major city. Important parts of the City of Brooklyn’s history are included, which include major immigration waves that contribute to the borough’s demographics today to population booms. This website gives insight to the borough’s modern form.

  12. Hurricane Sandy, one year after: Red Hook still Reeling and Recovering. (2013). Daily News. Retrieve http://www.nydailynews.com/new-york/hurricane-sandy/hurricane-sandy-year-red-hook-article-1.1494679

    The Daily News issued an article on how the people of Red Hook, New York were still dealing with theeffects of Hurricane Sandy. One year after the matter the Daily News the people report that the storm to them has not yet passed. Many had to throw away large amounts of personal possessions.

  13. Jones, Jenny. NYC Project Transforms Landfill into a Sprawling Recreational. (2011) Civil Engineering.

    This article written by Jenny Jones focuses on the transformation of the Fresh Kills Landfill into the new Fresh Kills park. It describes how the park was planned to divide into five main sections, and how each area would have their own plans for recreational activities. Another part of the journal focuses on the engineering process of how the landfill was going to be constructed into a beautiful park.

  14. Kiefer, M. (2001, January 1). Privatizing Creation of the Public Realm: The Fruits of New York City’s Incentive Zoning Ordinance. A Symposium: Jane Jacobs & The New Urban Ecology, 28 (Issue 4). Retrieved from http://lawdigitalcommons.bc.edu/ealr/vol28/iss4/10/

    An analysis performed on the reasoning, implementation and outcome of the Zoning Ordinance of 1961. This source is useful as it is a thoughtful analysis on the reason why the zoning ordinance was developed, and compares it to the zoning ordinance of 1916.

  15. Macdonald, E. (2005). Suburban Vision to Urban Reality: The Evolution of Olmsted and Vaux’s Brooklyn Parkway Neighborhoods. Journal of Planning History. Retrieved from http://jph.sagepub.com/content/4/4/295.full.pdf html

    This journal discusses Frederick Law Olmsted’s and Calvert Vaux’s plans for Brooklyn in the years preceding the borough’s consolidation with the City of New York. The article discusses their visions for the then-rural area’s urbanization, which includes landscape changes, such as Ocean Parkway and the design of several urban neighborhoods, that exist today. This article provides urban planning history in Brooklyn and allows one to understand some of the decisions that have contributed to the borough’s landscape.

  16. Melosi, M. (1981). Garbage in the cities: Refuse, reform, and the environment: 1880-1980 (p. 247). College Station, Texas: Texas A & M University Press.

    In the book by Melosi, he’s talking about George Waring was a leading key member of the sanitation department back in late 1800s. He talks about how Waring turn New York City into a cleaner city where it used to be one of the dirtiest city in the nation, and also how he became a hero of New York City.

  17. McNeur, C. (2014). Taming manhattan: environmental battles in the antebellum city. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press.

    An introductory recount on the development and transformation of Manhattan, beginning in the 1800s, a time of disarray where stray dogs and filth filled the streets. With Manhattan’s image, health, and property values on their minds, the upper class fought to create physical order, upgrade sanitation, redevelop neighborhoods and create parks. The lower class, however, became a battle for survival. This source is useful as it provides the baseline on how New York shapes their environmental development.

  18. Nagle, R. (2013). Picking up: On the streets and behind the trucks with the sanitation workers of New York City. New York: Farrar, Straus and Giroux

    Robin Nagle was named the Department of Sanitation of New York’s anthropologist-in-residence. This book is based off of Nagle’s research of the Sanitation Department, and includes first hand experience working for the department. The book provides many testimonies from sanitation workers, which gives a look into the eyes of the people who keep the streets of New York clean.

  19. Neckerman, K., Lovasi, G., Davies, S., Purciel, M., Quinn, J., Feder, E., ... Rundle, A. (2009). Disparities In Urban Neighborhood Conditions: Evidence From GIS Measures And Field Observation In New York City. Journal of Public Health Policy, S264-S285. Retrieved February 12, 2015, from http://www.palgrave- journals.com/jphp/journal/jphp200847a.html

    A field study that assessed different neighborhoods using GIS to link the correlation between physical walking and health related issues. Demonstrated that a municipality’s structure and built environment heavily influence the residents’ movement and choice to actively disperse within the city around them. Findings suggested some changes in policy to further progression of citizen movement, and implicated the technological advantages of using Geographic Information Systems to assess more than just mapping zones.

  20. Newman, K & Wyly, E. (July\August 2005). Gentrification and resistance in new york city. Shelterforce Online (issue #142). Retrieved from http://www.nhi.org/online/issues/142/gentrification.html

    Discusses the difficulty of quantifying the results of gentrification, such as displacement and neighborhood change. It also brings to light the benefits and the problems the poor faces when it comes to heavy market forces. This source is useful as it shows the dark side of neighborhoods which successfully went through redevelopment.

    The website is simply talking about the history of public transportation of New York City. The website was created by a non-profit organization in New York called Transit Museum. It gives us many pros and cons of each transit, and also history of these transits.
