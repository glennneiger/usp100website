Public Transit on the Streets


Horsecar

New York Trolley

Horsecars, it was a streetcar that was pull buy a horse in the front and the car on the steel track. It was the original concept of the cable cars. Horsecars are able to carry a large amount of people and the driver sat in the back, the passengers would ring the bell the signal the driver to stop at the stops. The horsecars are more comfortable than Omnibuses. However, the horses are slow and when it comes to the hills, the horses are having trouble to climb the hills. In addition, the horses need to consume a lot of hay and grain which also produce a lot of manure. The average of year of the horses that work as a horsecar is only five years. In 1872, the outbreak of horse flu had caused the death of many horses, this showed the New York City that they need to find a new power source for public transportation.

The first cable car of New york city was opened in 1883 on the new Brooklyn Bridge. Cable cars were different from horsecars, they are no longer powered by horses, instead they were moved by steam engine. Cable cars were a lot useful and more effective than the horsecars.
